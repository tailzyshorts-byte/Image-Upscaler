How to use LuminaStudio

Follow these simple steps to enhance and style your images

1

Open the app in Canva

Find LuminaStudio in the Canva Apps panel and click to open it.

2

Upload your image(s)

Click the upload zone and select any JPG, PNG, or WEBP images from your device. You can upload multiple images for bulk processing.

3

Choose your settings

Select your desired resolution upscale (2x to 8x), pick an artistic filter, and choose an optional creative frame.

4

Click Upscale & Apply

The app processes your image locally in the browser to apply enhancements, filters, and frames. This may take a few seconds depending on the file size.

5

Compare and add to design

For single images, drag the before/after slider to see the difference. For multiple images, select from the results gallery to automatically add them to your Canva design!

💡 Tips for best results

✅ Use 2× for most photos — best balance of quality and speed
✅ Works best on blurry or compressed images (JPEGs)
✅ The Vintage Film filter looks amazing with the Polaroid frame!
⚠️ Very large images at 8× upscale may take longer to process
⚠️ Already high-resolution images may only show subtle upscaling improvements

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to common questions

🔒

Is my image private?

Yes, 100%. All processing happens inside your browser. Your images are never uploaded to any server and never leave your device.

💰

Is it free?

Completely free. No signup, no subscription, no credits. All features are available to everyone at no cost.

⏱️

Why is it slow on 8×?

Higher scale factors process significantly more pixels. An 8× upscale processes 64× more data than the original. Use 2× or 4× for faster results.

📱

Does it work on mobile?

Yes, the app works on mobile devices. However, very large images at high scale factors may be slow on older phones.

🖼️

What image formats are supported?

JPG, PNG, and WEBP are supported. PNG gives the best quality output. JPEG output is used automatically for very large images to save file size.

🔄

Why does the app cap output sizes?

Very large images at 8× can exceed browser memory limits. The app automatically caps output at 16 megapixels to prevent crashes.

📐

Why is the image the same size on canvas?

By design! The image is placed at your original display size but with much higher resolution data inside — like a Retina display. This keeps your layout intact.

⬇️

Can I download the processed image?

Yes! After processing, click the "Download PNG" button to save the full-resolution, stylized image to your device.
